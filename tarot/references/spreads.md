# Spreads

Choose the smallest spread that can answer the user's question without making the reading vague.

## Single

Use for a daily reflection, quick emotional check-in, or a focused "what should I notice?" question.

- 核心提示: the central symbol or reflective prompt.

## Three-Card

Use as the default for most questions. It is clear, flexible, and not too long.

- 过去/背景: the context, pattern, or prior influence.
- 现在/核心: the live tension or central theme.
- 未来/可能走向: a possible direction if the current pattern continues.

Do not describe the third card as fixed fate.

## Relationship

Use for romantic, friendship, family, collaborator, or other interpersonal questions.

- 你的位置: the querent's stance, need, or blind spot.
- 对方的位置: the other person's visible stance or possible inner weather.
- 关系动力: the pattern between both sides.
- 阻碍或盲点: what is hard to see or name.
- 可尝试的行动: grounded next step within the querent's control.

Avoid mind-reading. Say "可能", "看起来像", or "如果这张牌映照的是对方状态".

If the user asks about people who are not present, keep the spread centered on the querent's experience, observations, boundaries, and next step. Do not claim hidden private facts.

## Celtic Cross

Use only when the user asks for a deep reading or the question spans multiple life domains.

- 现状
- 挑战
- 根基
- 近期过去
- 显意识目标
- 近期发展
- 自我位置
- 环境影响
- 希望与恐惧
- 整合趋势

Keep each position concise. The value is in the synthesis, not ten isolated mini-readings.
